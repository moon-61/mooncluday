My Month's Diary

October started differently, with a much-awaited break and the most special visit of the year. Between Solomon's Temple, graffiti, a giant aquarium and Bolivian arepas, here's everything I experienced in October...

Written by Moon With much love
01

Week 1: Mini-vacation and my mom's arrival

October 1 - 7

October started differently, with something I'd been waiting for since I arrived in Brazil: a break. Between modules, PUC-SP gives a kind of mini-vacation. They're not "real" vacations, more like a week of rest to breathe, reorganize and prepare for what's coming. This October I got ten days like that, and they were perfect because my mom decided to visit me right during that time.

Memorable moment

They were super intense and precious days. We did so many things I don't even know where to start. The best part was simply being with her after so long! üíú

One of the most impactful experiences was visiting Solomon's Temple. It's a tour organized by an evangelical church that recreates various biblical scenarios: from the tabernacle to details nobody would expect to find in Brazil. They even bring real olive trees; there was one that was 400 years old. I stood there in silence just looking. It was impressive.

Seeing a 400-year-old olive tree in person is something you can't explain properly. It's living history right in front of you.

That same week, Design Week was also starting at the university. Since I was with my mom, I didn't go to all the activities, but I really took advantage of the workshop I attended. It was about graffiti, but explained from the authenticity of urban art.

What I learned about graffiti:
  • If you paint on a wall with permission, it's muralism
  • If you do it without permission, with adrenaline and risk, that's graffiti
  • That simple difference completely changed my view of urban art

They showed us different styles, how the lines move, the structure, the logic behind each stroke. Then we did a mini workshop and I had my first real approach to practice. It fascinated me. I've always liked urban art, but that day I felt it more alive than ever.

Week 3: Bolivian arepas and karaoke

October 15 - 21

And that's what I did. I started going out more with my friends. One day we cooked Bolivian arepas at one of their houses. We made them with corn flour, milk, cheese, butter, a touch of salt and sugar. We let it rest and then cooked it in the pan. They turned out incredibly well.

üéØ Bolivian arepas recipe:
  • Corn flour
  • Milk
  • Cheese
  • Butter
  • A touch of salt and sugar
  • Secret: Let the dough rest before cooking!

We were so proud that we took pictures of the dough, the process and the result. Then we had an improvised karaoke and the next day we visited a SESC, which is like a huge cultural center. It had everything: library, pools, cafeteria, gym, workshops, art exhibitions.

In one of the areas, we saw a lady telling stories to children. Half jokingly I said: "What if we ask her to read to us too?" We ended up there for an hour, listening to children's stories. Another lady was playing ukulele, which added extra charm to the scene.

Special moment at SESC:

When she found out I was from Bolivia, she got very emotional. She said she had traveled there several times and was in love with the country, especially with cu√±ap√©, sonso and arepa. I told her that was exactly what we'd had for breakfast that day and it was a nice little moment I won't forget.
