My Month's Diary

September was one of those strange months where you feel like you did nothing... but at the same time did a ton. Between intense projects, UX discoveries, new friends and lots of homesickness, here's everything I experienced in September...

Week 1: Diving into the project

September 1 - 7

September was one of those strange months where you feel like you did nothing... but at the same time did a ton. With the start of my project, I was practically buried in classes every day. Between one task and another I ended up learning so much: heuristics, good usability practices, accessibility, comfort, data security... that whole universe I'd always seen superficially, but now finally understood for real.

Discovery moment

Finally everything started making sense! Heuristics weren't just pretty words anymore, they were real tools I could use. ðŸ’œ

One of the activities that made me laugh the most was a comparison between two similar apps. Most groups chose the typical: Mercado Livre vs. Amazon, Deezer vs. Spotify... those things everyone knows. But I knew everyone was going to do the same, so I proposed to my group to compare PedidosYa with iFood. And they said yes.

What I learned this week:
  • Nielsen's heuristics in practice (not just theory!)
  • Good usability practices that make a real difference
  • Accessibility isn't optional, it's essential
  • Data security needs to be in every design decision

It was a somewhat risky idea, but it turned out better than expected: ironically, PedidosYa ended up being much more intuitive and comfortable for users than iFood. I didn't expect that.

Sometimes taking a risk with a different choice is really worth it. The group loved the idea!

Week 3: New friendships in the K-pop world

September 15 - 21

Some time before, a friend had invited me to a K-pop meetup. I went without expecting much, maybe just to make one new friend... and I ended up with two more. Brazilian people are incredibly kind and outgoing; the more I get to know them, the more they surprise me.

  • They're incredibly kind

  • They make you feel at home quickly
  • Always willing to share their passions (like K-pop!)

Between activities, I kept advancing on my project. And although I didn't go out as much as in other months, every time I did go out I made the most of it. But the truth is I was very focused on finishing, polishing details, organizing deliveries... that kind of thing that eats up hours without you noticing.

Lesson about balance:

When you're focused on a project, it's easy to leave everything else aside. But the few moments I took to go out and see people were very worth it. Balance is important.

Walks without headphones became my decompression moment. Something so simple, but that made all the difference in the midst of so much hustle.

I discovered that sometimes the best remedy for stress is simply walking without rush, observing the city.
