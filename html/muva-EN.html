01 Discovering new worlds

First contact with Arduino and Unity:

This module was a big challenge for me, because I had to deal with completely new things, like programming for Arduino Uno. I had some experience with C++, but adapting it for Arduino was quite an adventure: I needed to recall functions, loops and registers, and learn to apply them in a different environment. Additionally, we were introduced to Unity Hub, which for me was a totally unknown world, a mix of design and programming with C#. I confess that, at first, I felt lost, but also excited about everything I could learn.

Module objective and project idea:

The module's objective was to create a system that integrated hardware and software, a physical object connected to an application capable of monitoring, processing and executing information. We needed to think about digitalization, user experience and how technology could interact with the real world. It was an intense module, but with the help of our professor and many learning resources, we managed to advance, understanding how circuits work, how to program sensors and how to work with C# in developing our application.

02 From concept to practice

Our project: the smart glove

Muva is a glove capable of recording wrist pressure and movement to prevent carpal tunnel syndrome. If the user maintained the same position or repeated movements for too long, the application would send notifications recommending breaks or specific exercises. My colleagues focused on the hardware and sensors, while another colleague and I worked on the application using Unity Hub. It was a big challenge to think about how to place all the components inside the glove without bothering the user and choosing the right materials to make it functional and comfortable.

Research and learning during the process:

A key element of the project was research, including interviews with physiotherapists, which helped us discard ideas that seemed promising, such as adding a vibration motor that, according to specialists, could worsen the carpal tunnel condition. Thanks to those interviews and the trial and error process, we achieved a more solid, safe and well-executed project. Learning about programming, electronics, design and ergonomics at the same time was an enormous challenge, but, in the end, seeing the result working made us very proud.
